INSTRUCTOR SUPPORT

University instructors are invited to offer a semester or quarter course on Machine Learning based on our lectures and supporting material. These lectures, given by Feynman Prize winning Professor Yaser Abu-Mostafa, are available online for free, and an instructor package (see below) will be provided also for free.

The instructor can choose how to use the online material. For example, you can apply a 'flipped class' model and use the online lectures just as a free "video textbook" for your students, while having discussions and interactive learning in class. This has been successfully tried at Caltech. Here are some guidelines for instructors:

  • Lecture Videos: The students can watch the lectures at home, and class time can focus on discussions. You can also project the video lectures in class including the recorded Q&A, or without the Q&A and use that time for live discussion in class (the latter was the model used at Caltech). The videos in HD format have excellent quality and work well even for a huge lecture hall. For a medium or small classroom, the lower-resolution versions from YouTube or iTunesU work well, too.

  • Instructor Package: We have instructor packages with lecture slides to organize class discussion, polished homeworks and final exam with solutions, and a solution manual for the textbook exercises and problems if you want to choose homework and exam problems from a bigger collection. Please send a request on university letterhead by postal mail to: Y. Abu-Mostafa, Caltech 136-93, Pasadena, CA 91125, USA.

  • Instructor Practice: For instructors who have not taught Machine Learning before and are interested in offering our course in this self-service mode, we suggest that they take the course in its full service mode themselves as a dry run for preparation.

  • By participating, you, your school, and everyone taking the course agree to the terms and conditions.

SEMESTER OR QUARTER MODES

  • If you teach a quarter-system course (9-10 weeks of instruction), you can use the 18 lectures as is and perhaps give a review class or two at the end. You can have your own Q&A and discussion session at the end of each lecture in lieu of the recorded Q&A. The above Instructor Package includes lecture slides to guide the discussion.

  • If you teach a semester-system course (13-15 weeks of instruction), you can use the lectures for the first 9 weeks, then follow with a practical project on machine learning or follow with your own material on a specialized topic (e.g., reinforcement learning, graphical models, or other learning models). You can alternatively give the course at a more relaxed pace by spreading out the lectures and inserting a recitation or homework discussion class after every 2 video-lecture classes.

  • If your students don't have the prerequisites (basic probability, matrices, and calculus) or need to be introduced to some programming platform (e.g., MatLab), you can start the course with lectures covering these topics at a basic level before proceeding with the Learning From Data lectures.
